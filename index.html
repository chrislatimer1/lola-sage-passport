📖 WHAT IS AN API? — the 60-second version

Think of an API like a waiter in a restaurant. You (an app) sit at the table. The kitchen (another app) has what you need. The waiter (API) takes your order, goes to the kitchen, and brings back your food. You never go in the kitchen. You don't need to know how it works in there — you just get your food.

That's literally it. When you tap "Sign in with Google" on an app, that app doesn't have your Google password. It uses Google's API — the waiter — to check you're real and bring back just enough info to let you in. Safe. Clean. Done.

the big picture
📖 JOBS IN TECH — it's not all coding in hoodies

Here's a secret: most people in tech don't write code all day. There are hundreds of roles in a tech company. Here are some of the main ones:

💻 engineering

Software Engineer

Builds and tests the actual product. Writes code. Fixes bugs. Makes things work. Can earn £28k–£130k+.

🎨 design

UX Designer

Makes sure the product is easy and enjoyable to use. Designs the screens, flows, and experience. Huge demand.

📊 data

Data Analyst

Turns raw numbers into insights. Helps the business make decisions. "What's actually happening?" — that's their job.

🔐 security

Cybersecurity

Protects the company from hackers. Finds weaknesses before the bad guys do. Massive shortage of talent right now.

📦 product

Product Manager

Decides what gets built and why. Talks to customers, talks to engineers, figures out what to prioritise. Very strategic.

🤝 commercial

Sales / Marketing

Gets the product in front of people. Tells the story. Closes deals. You don't need to code to work in tech at all.

your path
📖 AFTER GCSEs — the options (with honest numbers)

No one tells you this stuff clearly. Here it is:

🎓 UNIVERSITY
£60k+
average debt at graduation

But repayments are only 9% of earnings above ~£25k. On a £30k salary that's just £37.50/month. The debt doesn't feel big — but you also don't earn anything for 3+ years.

🔧 DEGREE APPRENTICESHIP
£0
debt. zero. none.

You earn £20k–£28k WHILE getting a full degree. Sage, BT, IBM offer these. You graduate with the same qualification as a uni student — but with 4 years of experience and £0 owed.

💰 the real maths — cumulative earnings by age 25
Apprentice (started age 16) ~£216,000
Degree Apprentice (started age 18) ~£162,000
Uni Graduate (started earning at 21) ~£72,000

*estimates based on avg starting salaries. the point: starting earlier = more money by your mid-20s, regardless of route.
